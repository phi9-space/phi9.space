# phi9.space Page-by-Page Copy Brief

## What matters now

The site needs content now.

The visual direction is already strong enough to proceed. The next bottleneck is authored copy and a disciplined choice of proof surface for each section.

Do not treat every page as a multimedia page.

Use this rule:

- `Video` when motion itself is the proof
- `Still image` when the artifact needs authority and presence
- `Diagram` when the idea is procedural, structural, or systemic
- `Table` when the proof is comparative, technical, or operational
- `Text-only` when language should carry the section

## Production order

### Write first

1. `Home`
2. `Data`
3. `Lab`
4. `Manifesto`

These pages establish the institutional front door, the operating model, the data layer, and the worldview surface.

### Write second

5. `Research index`
6. `About`
7. nested `Data` detail pages where needed

These pages can reuse more existing material and mainly need restructuring, tightening, and reframing.

### Do not spend time on now

- `Newsroom`
- `Collaborate` as a standalone page

`Newsroom` should be removed. Contact and demo should live as utility actions, not as a separate primary narrative page.

#### Open Problems

**Section label**

Open problems

**Intro**

Some bottlenecks are still obvious. The work is deciding which ones are structural and which ones are only temporary.

**Problem 1**

Data that carries intent, not just observation.

Most pipelines still capture what happened on screen, not what the body did, what the task required, or what the demonstration was trying to achieve.

**Problem 2**

Generalization from scarce real-world behavior.

Physical data is expensive, messy, and uneven. Scaling it requires better multiplication, not just more collection.

**Problem 3**

Evaluation that predicts transfer.

Benchmarks are useful only if they tell us what survives outside the benchmark.

**Problem 4**

Research systems that close the loop.

Capture, structure, training, and evaluation are still too often treated as separate problems.

#### Why data matters

Why data

**Body**

Physical AI is bottlenecked by data quality, not just model quality. What matters is not only how much data exists, but whether it preserves motion, intent, timing, task structure, and failure.

**Section label**

Data types

**Intro**

Phi9 is interested in data that survives contact with training and evaluation.

**Type 1**

Full-body motion and kinematic traces.

**Type 2**

Egocentric and synchronized visual observation.

**Type 3**

Task structure, action sequences, and episode boundaries.

**Type 4**

Metadata, object state, and quality signals.

**Type 5**

Evaluation traces, failure cases, and transfer results.

**Section label**

Capture

#### Sourcing networks

**Section label**

Sourcing networks

**Body**

The bottleneck is not one rig. It is a network that can source, collect, and maintain data across people, places, and tasks without collapsing quality.

#### Data quality and synchronization

**Section label**

Quality

**Body**

Good data is synchronized, structured, and inspectable. Timing drift, missing context, and weak metadata do not just reduce polish. They change what the model can learn.

**Section label**

Outputs

**Body**

### Working copy blocks

#### Title

**Title**

Toward physical intelligence.

#### Opening claim

**Paragraph 1**

Physical intelligence will not emerge from scale alone. It will emerge from better contact with the world: better data, better systems, better questions, and better ways of understanding what actually transfers from experiment to operation.

**Paragraph 2**

Phi9 exists to build that contact surface. We are interested in the infrastructures that make physical behavior legible to machines and useful to researchers, builders, and operators.

#### Section 1

**Heading**

The world has to be captured before it can be learned.

**Body**

Physical AI is constrained by what it can observe, how faithfully it can retain it, and how well that observation survives into training. Data is not an accessory to intelligence. It is one of its first conditions.

#### Section 2

**Heading**

Research infrastructure is part of the work.

**Body**

Capture pipelines, synchronization, metadata, evaluation, and data multiplication are not secondary plumbing. They shape what research becomes possible, what products become reliable, and what progress becomes repeatable.

#### Section 3

**Heading**

Artifacts matter because they keep ideas honest.

**Body**

We prefer systems, traces, diagrams, datasets, and working surfaces to inflated claims. The artifact is where the argument meets resistance.

#### Section 4

**Heading**

The goal is not spectacle. The goal is transfer.

**Body**

A benchmark matters only if it predicts behavior outside itself. A demonstration matters only if it can be reused. A system matters only if it reduces the distance between research and operation.

#### Section 5

**Heading**

Progress should remain legible.

**Body**

We want work that can be inspected, extended, and challenged. That means staying close to the real bottlenecks, publishing where useful, and building the kinds of systems that make the next questions clearer.
