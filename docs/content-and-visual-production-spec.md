# phi9.space Content and Visual Production Spec

## What the site needs now

Yes. The bottleneck is content now, not more layout experimentation.

Phi9 already has:

- a workable visual thesis
- a usable token and typography base
- a clear homepage structure direction
- enough research media to support `Research`

What it does not yet have is:

- homepage-grade institutional copy for the back half of the page
- enough authored visual assets for the institutional pages
- clear page-by-page content production priorities

This document is the production plan for that work.

## Production priority

### Build now

1. `Home`
2. `Lab`
3. `Data`
4. `Manifesto`
5. `Research index`

### Keep but do not prioritize

- `MoCap nested detail`
- `About footer utility`
- `Research article template`
- `Legal pages`

### Do not build now

- `Newsroom`
- `Collaborate` as a standalone page

## The rule for visual choice

Do not ask “should this page have video?”

Ask:

- what is the one strongest proof surface for this section?
- does motion help understanding or only add atmosphere?
- do we have a real asset, or would video expose the lack of one?

Use this decision rule:

- `Video` when motion itself is the evidence
- `Still image` when the artifact needs authority and clarity
- `Diagram` when the idea is structural or systemic
- `Table` when the proof is comparative or technical
- `Text-only` when the power comes from language and spacing

### Section-by-section content and visual guidance

#### Hero

##### Content needed

- one headline
- one 2 to 3 sentence support paragraph
- one primary CTA
- one secondary CTA

##### Content source

- edit existing, do not rewrite from zero

##### Visual format

- `Still image` or `diagrammatic composite`

##### Why

The homepage hero should not be video unless Phi9 has one excellent institutional-grade clip. Weak footage will lower the whole page. A strong still or authored composite is safer and more controlled.

##### What to create

- one hero visual showing either the rig, motion data, or a research-system composite

#### Capability Loop

##### Content needed

- section label
- one heading
- 4 short step titles
- 4 short descriptions
- 4 visual captions

##### Content source

- mostly reuse existing

##### Visual format

- `Sticky diagram + still frames`

##### Why

This section is about system logic. It should not be a video block. Each state should feel like a page from a research deck: one image, one caption, one stage.

##### What to create

- 4 visual states for `Capture / Multiply / Train / Evaluate`

#### Featured Artifact

##### Content needed

- small label
- artifact name
- one paragraph
- one proof caption strip
- one CTA

##### Content source

- adapt from `mocap-rig.md`

##### Visual format

- `Still image first`, optional `short embedded demo video` second

##### Why

This section should feel tactile. Lead with a still or a set of stills. Add video only if it clearly shows what cannot be explained in a still.

##### What to create

- one strong hero still of the rig or system
- optional 10 to 20 second silent loop if the motion genuinely teaches something

#### Research Proof

##### Content needed

- one section heading
- one short framing sentence
- 1 featured research item
- 2 to 3 secondary research items

##### Content source

- reuse and tighten from existing posts

##### Visual format

- `Figure crops`, `graphs`, `annotated stills`

##### Why

Research proof should not be generic blog thumbnails. It should feel like evidence surfaces.

##### What to create

- choose one figure or media frame per highlighted post

#### Open Problems

##### Content needed

- section label
- one short intro
- 3 to 5 open problems
- each problem gets:
  - one-line tension
  - one clarifying sentence

##### Content source

- net new

##### Visual format

- `Text + diagrammatic side panel`

##### Why

This is a thinking section. It should not rely on video. Use diagrams, failed attempts, benchmark fragments, motion traces, and sparse annotation.

##### What to create

- 3 to 5 problem statements
- one side visual system that can change per problem or stay constant

#### Methods

##### Content needed

- section label
- section thesis
- 4 method stages
- one sentence each

##### Content source

- net new, but derived from existing lab/capability material

##### Visual format

- `Diagram` or `annotated system strip`

##### Why

Methods is not a film. It is a way-of-working section. Use diagrams, process fragments, schema, notes, and system artifacts.

##### What to create

- one stitched visual explaining how Phi9 moves from question to system

#### Principles

##### Content needed

- section label
- intro sentence
- 4 to 6 principles
- one consequence sentence each

##### Content source

- net new, but based on existing beliefs and prior notes

##### Visual format

- `Text-first`

##### Why

This section should be typographic. Images should be minimal and secondary.

##### What to create

- principle statements only
- optional margin annotations

#### Collaboration CTA

##### Content needed

- invitation headline
- one sentence on who Phi9 wants to talk to
- one primary CTA
- one secondary CTA

##### Content source

- new

##### Visual format

- `Text-only`

##### Why

End quietly. This page should close with institutional confidence, not a campaign banner.

## Minimum viable content sequence

If the goal is to get a strong v1 overhaul live, do this in order:

1. homepage hero support copy
2. homepage `Open Problems`
3. homepage `Methods`
4. homepage `Principles`
5. data page thesis and structure
6. lab hero and infrastructure copy
7. manifesto draft
8. research index cleanup

## Minimum viable asset sequence

If the goal is to make the redesign believable, create these assets first:

1. one strong homepage hero still or composite
2. 4 capability-loop visual states
3. one strong MoCap rig still
4. one MoCap anatomy diagram
5. one homepage `Methods` diagram
6. 3 to 5 visual cues for `Open Problems`

## Final decision rule

If there is no real asset yet:

- use text + diagram
- or text-only

Do not fill important pages with decorative placeholders.

On Phi9, weak video is worse than no video. Strong stills and authored diagrams are the safer default for the institutional pages.
